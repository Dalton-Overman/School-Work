WAS THIRD STRAIGHT RECORD YEAR FOR THE COMPANY</font></div>
      <div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br></div>
      <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">Pitman, New Jersey&#160;&#160;- -
March 5, 2008 </font>- -&#160;&#160;K-Tron International, Inc. (NASDAQ-GS: KTII)
today reported the best quarterly and full year results in its history, with
record revenues, net income and earnings per share for both the fourth quarter
and full year of 2007.&#160;&#160;Fourth quarter 2007 net income was $6.259
million, 40.3 percent higher than the $4.461 million reported in the fourth
quarter of 2006, on a 33.1 percent increase in year-over-year fourth quarter
revenues from $44.366 million to $59.055 million.&#160;&#160;Diluted earnings
per share rose 38.9 percent to $2.18 from $1.57 in the same period last
year.</font></div>

      <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">For the
full year 2007, K-Tron reported record annual net income of $21.321 million,
diluted earnings per share of $7.49 and revenues of $201.68 million compared to
record 2006 net income of $12.872 million, EPS of $4.59 (diluted) and revenues
of $148.13 million.&#160;&#160;The percentage increases in 2007 versus 2006&#8217;s
numbers were 65.6 percent for net income, 63.2 percent for EPS (diluted) and
36.2 percent for revenues.</font></div>
      <div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br></div>
      <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">In its
announcement, the Company noted that if the average foreign currency exchange
rates for the fourth quarter and full year of 2006 were applied to the same
periods in 2007, the Company&#8217;s revenues would have increased approximately 29.5
percent for the fourth quarter instead of 33.1 percent and 33.8 percent for the
full year instead of 36.2 percent, primarily due to a weaker U.S. dollar against
the Swiss franc, the euro and other relevant foreign currencies in this year&#8217;s
fourth quarter and full year compared to the same periods last
year.</font></div>

earnings in our history.&#160;&#160;We also completed two important acquisitions
