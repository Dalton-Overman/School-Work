<DIV align="center" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt"><B>K-TRON REPORTS THIRD QUARTER 2009 RESULTS</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="Center" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt"><B>STRONG OPERATING CASH FLOW PRODUCES $13.357 MILLION INCREASE<BR>
IN CASH AND $2.0 MILLION REDUCTION IN DEBT DURING THE QUARTER</B>

</DIV>
<DIV align="justify" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt"><B>Pitman, New Jersey &#151;  November&nbsp;9, 2009 </B> &#151; K-Tron International, Inc. (NASDAQ-GS: KTII) today
reported net income of $6.768&nbsp;million and diluted earnings per share (&#147;EPS&#148;) of $2.34 for its
fiscal third quarter ended October&nbsp;3, 2009, nearly unchanged from the same period in 2008.
Revenues were $47.321&nbsp;million in this year&#146;s third quarter, a decline of 20.6&nbsp;percent from last
year.
</DIV>

<DIV align="justify" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt">For the first nine months of 2009, K-Tron reported net income of $16.473&nbsp;million, diluted EPS of
$5.75 and revenues of $147.044&nbsp;million, compared to net income of $19.576&nbsp;million, diluted EPS of
$6.84 and revenues of $177.239&nbsp;million in the first nine months of 2008. The percentage decreases
in 2009 versus the prior year were 15.9&nbsp;percent for net income, 15.9&nbsp;percent for EPS and 17.0
percent for revenues.
</DIV>

<DIV align="justify" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt">The third quarter and first nine months of 2009 results included a $2.972&nbsp;million pre-tax gain from
the September&nbsp;2009 sale of the Company&#146;s 19.9&nbsp;percent equity investment in a French material
handling company (which investment had been recorded as an &#147;other asset&#148; on the Company&#146;s balance
sheet). Also, net income and EPS in both 2009 periods were adversely affected by higher effective
income tax rates this year as compared to 2008 (35.0&nbsp;percent for the third quarter and 34.7&nbsp;percent
for the first nine months of 2009 versus 27.1&nbsp;percent and 28.7&nbsp;percent for the same periods last
year).
</DIV>

<DIV align="justify" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt">The Company noted that if the average foreign currency exchange rates for the third quarter and
first nine months of 2008 were applied to the same periods of 2009, the Company&#146;s revenues would
have decreased approximately 20.5&nbsp;percent for the third quarter of 2009 instead of 20.6&nbsp;percent and
15.3&nbsp;percent for the first nine months of 2009 instead of 17.0&nbsp;percent.
</DIV>

<DIV align="justify" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt">With respect to liquidity and capital resources, the Company highlighted a significant increase in
cash and a reduction in debt during the third quarter, noting that cash grew by $13.357&nbsp;million to
$63.0&nbsp;million while debt was paid down by $2.0&nbsp;million to $18.0&nbsp;million. As a result, the Company
had cash in excess of debt of $45.0&nbsp;million at the end of the third quarter. The Company also said
that it had retired an additional $8.0&nbsp;million of debt in October and expects to end the year with debt of
only $8.0&nbsp;million as compared to $23.662&nbsp;million at the beginning of the year.
</DIV>

<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><!-- Folio -->&nbsp;<!-- /Folio -->
</DIV>

<DIV style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',Times,serif; margin-left: .25in; width: 7.20in">


<DIV align="justify" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt">Commenting on the Company&#146;s performance, K-Tron Chairman and Chief Executive Officer Edward B.
Cloues, II said, &#147;The third quarter of 2009 was a very positive one for the Company, despite the
challenging global environment. Our net income and EPS, helped by the sale of our investment in a
French material handling company, matched last year&#146;s record third quarter numbers, and our strong
operating cash flow enabled us to pay down debt while increasing our cash to a record $63.0
million.
</DIV>

<DIV align="justify" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt">&#147;Although our backlog declined by $5.0&nbsp;million, or 9.1%, in the third quarter on a constant foreign
currency exchange basis, we believe that our bookings may have bottomed out in the quarter and
expect that they will begin to grow again in the fourth quarter. While we still do not anticipate
any significant recovery before mid-2010 in many of our key markets, we are encouraged by how
relatively well we have performed this year under very difficult circumstances and look forward to
a gradually improving business environment. We also believe, as we said last quarter, that the
cost reduction initiatives implemented by us earlier in the year will enable the Company to
continue to deliver good earnings and cash flow despite the tough conditions we still face, and our
strong cash balance, good cash flow and low-cost borrowing availability position us well to take
advantage of acquisition and other growth opportunities that may present themselves as the global
economy begins to recover.&#148;
</DIV>

<DIV align="justify" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt">On another matter, the Company noted that for the third straight year it had been named to <I>Forbes
Magazine&#146;s </I>list of the 200 Best Small Companies. K-Tron was ranked number 75 this year, following
rankings of 13 and 19 in 2008 and 2007. Candidates must have annual revenue between $5&nbsp;million and
$750&nbsp;million, be publicly traded for at least a year and have a stock price no lower than $5.
Rankings are based on earnings growth, sales growth and return on equity in the past 12&nbsp;months and
over five years.
</DIV>

<DIV align="justify" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt">K-Tron International, Inc. and its subsidiaries design, produce, market and service material
handling equipment and systems for a wide variety of industrial markets. The Company has
manufacturing facilities in the United States, Switzerland and the People&#146;s Republic of China, and
its equipment is sold throughout the world.
</DIV>
