<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt">
<br></div>
<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="center"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">STRONG CASH FLOW PRODUCES $6.531 MILLION INCREASE IN CASH AND $2.602 MILLION REDUCTION IN DEBT DURING THE QUARTER</font></div>
<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt">
<br></div>
<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: -4.5pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">Pitman, New Jersey - - August 10, 2009 </font>- -&#160;&#160;K-Tron International, Inc. (NASDAQ-GS: KTII) today reported net income of $5.258 million and diluted earnings per share (&#8220;EPS&#8221;) of $1.82 for its fiscal second quarter ended July 4, 2009.&#160;&#160;This represented
a 26.5 percent decrease in net income and a 26.9 percent decrease in diluted EPS from the same period in 2008.&#160;&#160;Revenues were $50.037 million in this year&#8217;s second quarter, a decline of 16.9 percent from last year.&#160;&#160;These results reflected weaker sales and unfavorable foreign currency exchange rates compared to 2008&#8217;s second quarter, which was the best quarter in the Company&#8217;s history, while net income and EPS were also adversely affected by a higher effective income tax
rate this year (35.5 percent versus 28.5 percent in last year&#8217;s second quarter).</font></div>
<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt">
<br></div>
<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">For the first half of 2009, K-Tron reported net income of $9.705 million, diluted EPS of $3.37 and revenues of $99.723 million, compared to net income of $12.809 million, diluted EPS of $4.47 and revenues of $117.608 million in the first half of 2008.&#160;&#160;The percentage decreases in 2009 versus the prior year were 24.2 percent for
net income, 24.6 percent for EPS and 15.2 percent for revenues.</font></div>
<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt">
<br></div>
<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: -4.5pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">The Company noted that if the average foreign currency exchange rates for the second quarter and first six months of 2008 were applied to the same periods of 2009, the Company&#8217;s revenues would have decreased approximately 14.7 percent for the second quarter of 2009 instead of 16.9 percent and 12.7 percent for the first six months
of 2009 instead of 15.2 percent.&#160;&#160;&#160;The differences were primarily due to a stronger U.S. dollar against the Swiss franc, the euro and other relevant foreign currencies in this year&#8217;s second quarter and first half compared to the same periods last year.</font></div>
<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt">
<br></div>
<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: -4.5pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">&#8220;K-Tron posted a good second quarter performance in a challenging global environment,&#8221; said the Company&#8217;s Chairman and Chief Executive Officer, Edward B. Cloues, II.&#160;&#160;&#8220;While our net income and EPS were well below last year&#8217;s record numbers, both were up 18.2 percent over the first quarter of this
year on a less than one percent increase in revenues.&#160;&#160;We have worked hard to control costs, and our success in this effort is reflected in our improved second quarter results versus the first quarter.&#160;&#160;</font></div>
<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: -4.5pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">&#160;</font></div>
<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: -4.5pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">&#160;</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: -4.5pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">Our global employee headcount is down nearly 10 percent from the beginning of the year, and we have also implemented selective furloughs and shorter work schedules in our operations most affected by lower volumes.&#8221;</font></div>
<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt">
<br></div>
<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: -4.5pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">Mr. Cloues commented that the Company&#8217;s order backlog declined by $6.242 million, or 10.4%, in the second quarter on a constant foreign currency exchange basis, and, looking ahead, he said that &#8220;K-Tron does not expect any recovery in 2009 in many of its key markets.&#160;&#160;As a result, we have extended our existing worldwide
salary and wage freeze through the end of the first quarter of 2010, and the salaries of our five executive officers have been reduced by 8 percent for the second half of this year.&#160;&#160;With these and the other aggressive cost actions we have taken, we believe that the Company is well-positioned to continue to deliver good earnings and cash flow despite the tough conditions we still face.&#8221;</font></div>
